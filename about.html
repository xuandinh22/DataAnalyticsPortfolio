About Me

My name is Xuan Dinh (Ophelia). I am passionate about uncovering patterns in data, much like a detective piecing together clues to solve a case. With a strong foundation in Economics and Statistics, I enjoy analyzing information to reveal trends, solve complex problems, and support decisions that create real-world impact. I see the role of a data analyst as the perfect intersection of analytical rigor and creative problem-solving, turning complexity into clarity. In the long term, I aspire to work in data analytics consulting, providing actionable guidance that helps organizations achieve lasting results.

Strengths

One of my greatest strengths is adaptability. I was an international student when I first came to the United States, leaving behind my family and everything familiar. Those early days were challenging and eye-opening, teaching me to step outside my comfort zone, embrace uncertainty, and approach every problem with curiosity and an open mind. I carry that mindset into my work, seeing data not just as numbers, but as stories to be discovered and translated into strategic recommendations.

I also describe myself as both a dreamer and a doer. Dreaming fuels creativity, helping me explore problems from multiple perspectives and generate innovative ideas. Doing keeps me grounded, ensuring I transform ideas into tangible solutions that deliver measurable results.

Education & Skills

I earned a degree in Economics with a focus on quantitative analysis, and a minor in Statistics, from San Diego State University. To further strengthen my analytical capabilities, I have completed courses including Tableau Business Intelligence Analyst, Excel Skills for Business, Google Data Analytics, and SQL through LearnSQL.com. These experiences have equipped me with hands-on proficiency in SQL, Excel, and Tableau, allowing me to extract meaningful patterns and communicate them effectively to support informed decision-making.
